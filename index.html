Proposition: The NYPD is (or is not) held adequately accountable for civilian complaints

FOR the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • [2] Data Transformations: The dataset was grouped by the year in which each complaint was received, and the total number of unique complaints (complaint_id) was calculated for each year. The number of distinct officers (unique_mos_id) involved in complaints during the same period was then determined. Dividing the total number of complaints by the number of officers ecah year produced the average number complaints per officer, providing a normalized measure that accounts for fluctuations in the size of the police force over time
  • [2] Downward Trend Line: A linear trend line was added to highlight the overall decline in average complaints per officer over time, drawing attention to the long-term pattern rather than year-to-year fluctuations
  • [-1] Truncating the Y-Axis: The y-axis begins at 0.8 instead of 0 to visually accentuate the decrease in complaint rates. This design choice makes the downard trend appear steeper, enhancing the visual impression of improvement over time
  • [1.5] Dropped Values: Data from 2020 was omitted due to incomplete records, as complaints were collected only during the early months of that year. Including this partial data would have created a misleading spike in the average complaints per officer. Removing it ensures the visualization maintains a consistent comparison across years
  • [2] Annotations: Text labels were added to mark the peak and lowest points of the trend. These annotations guide viewers toward the most significant moments in the data, reinforcing the main proposition that the NYPD is held accountable for civilian complaints
